Case Study #1 Expert: A UX Study in Connecting Users to Professionals

Overview

Everyone experiences moments in their life where they need or want professional help. Professional help can range from learning a hobby to repairing a car. Although there are more ways to connect with a professional than ever before, sometimes the professional help that is given isn't helpful or sufficient. As part of my time at Careerfoundry, I took on the challenge of creating the app Expert to address this problem.

Timeline: September 2021 - April 2022

Role: UX Researcher and UX Designer

Problem Statement

Users need a way to communicate with experts at a given time in a productive manner because they lack important problem solving information only experts can provide.

Objective

Create an app which will allow users to communicate with experts at a given time and be satisfied with their time and money spent.

Challenges

  1. Users might need to contact experts at a time that doesn't coincide with the time of the experts.
  2. Experts might insufficiently answer user questions, thus wasting the time and money of the user.
  3. In certain circumstances there might be a conflict between demand and supply. Perhaps the demand for an expert in one area might be too niche for example.
  4. The qualifications for an expert might be too subjective in certain areas such as a video game fighter professional.
  5. Maybe an expert or user might share confidential information with a 3rd party

Goals

  1. Create an app that provides a satisfactory experience to both users and experts.
  2. Create a filter system that will provide users more options to search for experts of any topic.
  3. Be a trustworthy company that is known for ethics and high standard of quality.
  4. Be accessible to both users and experts. Make the experience affordable for users and profitable for experts.

Competitive Analysis

To help Expert stand out, I started competitive analysis in which I focused on two freelancer companies known as Fiverr and Freelancer. Since my project focuses on connecting users to experts for help, I thought that focusing on other apps that focus on connecting between users and professional freelancers would allow me to identify their strengths and weaknesses. Analyzing and identifying these companies' weaknesses and strengths would be crucial for Expert to breakout into the market.

Usability Testing

After I finished my high-fidelity prototype, I scouted for six participants to participate in a test analysis using a test script I created. After I recruited my test participants, I moderated the test through Zoom and recorded our testing session with their consent. After I finished the testing session, I reviewed the footage and took notes. I then organized my notes into an affinity map to clearly organize my thoughts.

Usability Testing Results

After creating my affinity map, I created a rainbow spreadsheet to further organize my notes and I used the Jakob's rating scale to evaluate their experience based off of the affinity map results. Based on where their experience landed on Jakob's rating scale, I decided what need to be changed before creating the final mockup.

Usablity Test Report

Usability testing reports show the thought process of the participants who test out the prototype and the missteps of the app. In this case, the usability testing reports were separated into 3 different categories: issues, suggested changes, and evidence. The example below is one of the usability test reports that was shown.

Issue: Participants didn't understand the message box icon (low)

Suggested change: Change the message box icon and replace it with a more recognizable message box icon.

Evidence: Some of the participants were initially confused from the message box icon. They were quick to recognize that the icon was meant to represent the messages, but they were confused why I chose that icon to do so.

Designing for Accessibility

After completing all of the main functions of the Expert app, it was time for me to add the final touches. One of the most important parts was to design for accessibility. One of the issues that I noticed was that the messages page had an issue conveying to the user whether or not the expert was online as well as which messages the user had read or not. To remedy this situation I added the words "Active Now" below the icon to indicate whether or not an expert was online. In addition, I only used colors to tell whether a message had been read or not. I made certain to add the words read and unread with bold white text to draw the users attention to whether the most recent message had been read.

Project Take Aways and Prototype Link

Focusing on the User

As a UX designer, I am passionate about all of my design choices. I ensure that all of my design choices are based on some principle of design. Despite how confident and how proud I am of my design choices, there will always be times when further improvement or changes are required of my designs. When a difference of opinion occurs with the user or client, I make certain to explain my design choices while being ready to make any necessary changes even though it may be difficult. I take the feedback and criticisms and use them as an opportunity to improve upon my future design choices.

Accessibility

At times I take for granted is a product's accessibility. Some people are born with certain disabilities such as being color-blind which can cause a major accessibility issues in a product. For example, if an app relies solely on color to signal to users, then a color-blind person will suffer while using said app. Realizing this made me realize how important it is for products to be as accessible to everyone as possible. Even language plays a role in whether a product will be accessible for someone.

UX Design is based on a story

Anytime a product is created, there is a story behind it with a problem being the starting point. The nail was created since someone at some point in human history needed to bind material together. Ships were created so that humans could travel bodies of water to improve trade or search for new lands of travel. Every product that I work on is based on a problem and it is my job as a UX Designer to show the story of how this product came to be based on UX Design principles.
